Muzammil-E-Haque Mahmud

Early Life

Muzammil-E-Haque Mahmud is a Pakistani student currently studying at Washington University in St. Louis pursing a degree in Computer Science and Engineering. A Junior at WASHU Muzammil is the son of well known Professor of Rheumatologist, Dr. Tafazzul-E-Haque Mahmud and Professor of Radiology, Dr. Mah Jabeen Masood. Born into a family of doctors and dentists, Muzammil is the first to pursue a career in engineering. He was born on 29th August, 1997 in the famous City of Gardens, Lahore in the Punjab province of Pakistan. Muzammil-E-Haque Mahmud He was then enrolled into Aitchison College where he completed Elementary, Middle, and High School.

Time in Aitchison College

Elementary and Prepatory School

Muzammil was from an early age very much involved in Sports at Aitchison College. In elementary school, he excelled in Soccer, Horse Riding, Taekwondo, Hockey, and Track Field. In Preparatory School, he decided to focus on Soccer as his main sport, and later became the captain of his preparatory school's varsity soccer team.

High School

In High School, Muzammil become the highest goal scorer of his high school's varsity soccer team, setting a record. He captained his high school team for two years and was in his senior year recruited into the Pakistan National Soccer Team.

Apart from sports, Muzammil was heavily involved in extra curricular activities. He was a part of the College's Math Society, Debating Team, MUN Society, Acturial Sciences Club, and the Quiz Society. In high school, Muzammil took up STEM courses, and was very interested in Mathematics. His love for Mathematics and Puzzle solving paved his way for puring a degree in computer science and engineering.

Goals, Ambitions and Other Interests

Goals and Ambitions

Growing up around doctors and dentists, Muzammil plans on combining the fields of computer science and medicine together to bring about medical advancements using his computer science techniques. Muzammil is currently working on developing a framework for a generic context aware real time systems that would be compatible with many real time system algorithms. He later plans to use this framework on medical technology specially in the field of Rheumatology.
